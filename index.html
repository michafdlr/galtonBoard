Galton Board

This is a simulation of the Galton Board. $n\in\mathbb N$ represents the number of peg rows (i.e. the number of trials), and $p \in (0,1)$ is the probability of a ball falling to the right side. If the random variable $X$ describes how often a ball falls to the right side then $X$ has binomial distribution and the probability for falling $k\in\mathbb N\cap[0, n]$ times to the right side is given by $\mathbb P(X=k) = \binom nk \cdot p^k\cdot (1-p)^{n-k}$.

For the simulation $n$ can be a number from 1 to 9, and the number of balls from 1 to 500 inclusive. The simulation speed can be set from 0.5 to 2 in step sizes of 0.5.
